## Option A: data + bilingual angle (recommended)

I just shipped 26 posts on BorderPulse, every one paired English and Spanish (13 + 13).

The hook: a CBP wait time is a number. A 30-day median by hour is a plan.

For example, Otay Mesa's median northbound wait over the last 30 days is about 100 minutes. Cross around 9 AM and the median drops to roughly 55. Same port, same week, the hour you pick is about half the wait. Most people choose by habit instead of by the data.

Each post:
- Reads from real CBP snapshots, not guesses
- Embeds an interactive heatmap of the actual last 30 days
- Links every program rule (SENTRI, Ready Lane, FMM) straight to the official source
- Has a Spanish twin in native fronterizo voice, not Google Translate

Coverage so far: San Ysidro, Otay Mesa, Paso del Norte, Bridge of the Americas, Nogales DeConcini, plus head-to-head comparisons and program guides. CA, AZ, TX corridors.

The bilingual move was overdue. The big "Como esta la linea" Facebook groups exist because Google's Spanish shelf for border content is mostly empty. That is the gap I am trying to fill.

## Option B: shorter, builder angle

26 new posts on BorderPulse, all bilingual, all grounded in the actual last 30 days of CBP data.

The thing I keep finding: at the same port, the gap between a light crossing and a brutal one is mostly the hour you arrive. Most people do not know which hour. The data does.

Every post is paired EN + ES. Native Spanish voice, Mexican and fronterizo, not machine translated. Every program rule links to the official source.
