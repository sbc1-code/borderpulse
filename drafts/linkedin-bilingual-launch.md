## Option A: data + bilingual angle (recommended)

I just shipped 12 data-driven blog posts on BorderPulse, all bilingual.

The hook: a CBP wait time is a number. A 30-day median by hour and day of week is a strategy.

For example, Otay Mesa on Saturdays at 9 AM has a median wait of 0 minutes over the last 30 days. Monday at 4 AM hits 240 minutes. Same port, 240x difference, same week. Most people pick by habit instead of by data.

Each post:
- Reads from real CBP snapshots, not estimates
- Embeds an interactive heatmap of the actual 30 days
- Links every program rule (SENTRI, Ready Lane, FMM) directly to the official source
- Has a Spanish twin with native fronterizo voice, not Google Translate

Coverage so far: San Ysidro, Otay Mesa, Paso del Norte, Bridge of the Americas, Nogales DeConcini, plus comparison posts. CA, AZ, TX corridors.

The bilingual move was overdue. The 430,000-member "Cómo está la línea Tijuana" Facebook group exists because Google's Spanish shelf for border content is mostly empty. That's the gap I'm trying to fill.
