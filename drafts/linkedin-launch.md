If you've ever sat in a 2-hour line at San Ysidro wondering if Otay Mesa was faster, or driven to Bridge of the Americas only to find out Ysleta had no wait... yeah, same. I grew up crossing the border between El Paso, Juarez, San Diego, and Tijuana. That guessing game never made sense to me.

So I built something: borderpulse.com

It pulls official CBP wait time data for 43 crossings across California, Arizona, New Mexico, and Texas, updated every 15 minutes. It also estimates southbound delays (US to Mexico), which CBP doesn't publish at all.

A few things that make it different:

- Fully bilingual, English and Spanish
- Southbound delay estimates at major crossings
- No app to download, works on any phone browser
- No accounts, no cookies, no tracking
- Free and open source

This is a personal project, not a company. I built it because the border is part of my life, and the tools that existed were either outdated, English-only, or covered one direction.

If you cross regularly, or know someone who does: borderpulse.com

I'd love feedback from anyone in the cross-border community.
